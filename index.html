Who's the Best Captain? Depends on Your Criteria...


In this somewhat personal and totally not embarrassing site we'll be comparing and contrasting the captains of the Star Trek Silver Age. Between 1987 and 2005, Brannon Braga and Gene Roddenberry dropped four straight BANGERS on tv audiences. Star Trek: The Next Generation, Star Trek: Deep Space Nine, Star Trek: Voyager, and Star Trek: Enterprise. The captains are, respectively, Captain Jean-Luc Picard, Captain Benjamin Sisko, Captain Katheryn Janeway, and Captain Jonathan Archer. We will be judging the captains based on five basic criteria: Command Style, Personal Style, Hobbies, Relationships, and ✨Miscellaneous✨.


Captain Jean-Luc Picard:

Command style:

Picard commands from a bit of a distance He clearly cares about his crew, but he doesn't form particularly personal relationships, aside from with Dr Beverly Crusher (put i a page jump link). He starts out pretty stern, but quickly warms. He has a self-assuridness that inspires confidence, rather than giving off arrogant vibes. And the few times he’s caught off guard he’s deeply charming.

Personal style:

Picard loves a flowy blouse and jodhpurs, reflecting his archeological background. And also like, these jammies, pictured above. Which slapp harder than any future bro pj’s deserve to. Hobbies:

Can we assume that Picard, and in general the archeological community of the future have worked to be ethical in their studies, yeah. Is his love for archeology a weakness that leads him to have adventures with a smokin' hot black market baddie? Also yeah.
